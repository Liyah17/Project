Introduction of the personal computer

The introduction of the personal computer, often credited to companies like Apple and IBM in the late 1970s and early 1980s, marked a paradigm shift in computing. These compact and user-friendly machines democratized access to computing power, empowering individuals and businesses with tools for productivity, creativity, and communication. The widespread adoption of personal computers laid the foundation for the digital age, revolutionizing how information is processed, stored, and shared on a global scale.
