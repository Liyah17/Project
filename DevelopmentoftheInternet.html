Development of the Internet

The development of the internet traces back to the late 1960s with the creation of ARPANET, a project initiated by the U.S. Department of Defense's Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA). This network aimed to facilitate communication between research institutions. Over subsequent decades, ARPANET evolved into the modern internet through the development of protocols like TCP/IP and the World Wide Web, fostering global connectivity and revolutionizing how people access information, communicate, and conduct business worldwide.
